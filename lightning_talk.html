SmallWorlds

A network of community-operated digital hubs offering a suite of privacy-respecting, everyday software applications.

Why SmallWorlds?

Big Tech rents you access to your own digital life. SmallWorlds hands it back.

🔑 Own your apps & your data

Your photos, files, mail and code live on infrastructure you control — not on someone else's servers, under someone else's terms of service that can change overnight.

🛡️ Privacy & control

No profiling, no data mining, no silent lock-in. You decide who gets access and when. Your identity and encryption keys are yours to govern.

🚫 No advertisements

You are the owner, not the product. No ads, no upsells, no essential feature hidden behind a "premium" paywall.

✨ One seamless suite

A full set of everyday apps behind a single sign-on and one dashboard — the convenience of Big Tech, minus the surveillance and the subscription creep.

The Vision: A small world...

... that everyone can setup and operate.

Suitable for you, your family, your community, etc.

Rapid Setup

Fully automated installation: from zero to fully operational multi-app infrastructure and DNS setup in ~20 minutes.

Central Identity Management

Single Sign On, Passkey Support, Private email.

Sovereign Cloud

You own the apps and data. Can be hosted on any cloud provider or at home.

Lifecycle Management

GitOps version control. ArgoCD synchronizes cluster. Automated updates and backups. Add your own Apps.

Why you should NOT use SmallWorlds 🙈

In the interest of full honesty, some excellent reasons to run away:

🧑‍🔧 You are the IT department

When it breaks at 2 a.m., there is no support hotline. There is only you, a terminal, and a mirror to blame.

🐣 It's a prototype

Held together by YAML, caffeine, and one person's stubbornness. Here be dragons — and the occasional CrashLoopBackOff.

🤷 No more good excuses

"Sorry, the cloud is down" stops working when the cloud is a little box you named and put in a datacenter yourself.

😎 You might actually enjoy it

Warning: side effects include strong opinions about Kubernetes and boring your friends at parties with the word "GitOps".
